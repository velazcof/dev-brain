
Python Standards are the **set of formal specifications, conventions, and proposals that define the behavior, evolution, and best practices of the Python language and its ecosystem**.

They ensure consistency across implementations, guide language development, and establish common rules for writing and structuring Python code.

---
### `Key Ideas`

- Defined primarily through the **Python Enhancement Proposal (PEP) system**
    
- Govern both **language features** and **coding conventions**
    
- Ensure consistency across **Python implementations and tooling**
    
- Cover areas such as:
    
    - language syntax and semantics
    - standard library design
    - packaging and distribution
    - coding style and formatting
    
- Maintained and reviewed by the **Python core development community**
    
- Include both **mandatory specifications** and **recommended guidelines**
