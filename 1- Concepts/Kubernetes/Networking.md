
Networking refers to the systems and mechanisms responsible for **communication between applications, services, containers, and external clients within a Kubernetes cluster**. It enables workloads to discover, expose, and exchange data with each other across distributed environments.

---
### `Key Ideas`

- **Service communication**  
    Kubernetes networking enables communication between pods, services, and external systems through cluster networking abstractions.

- **Traffic routing and exposure**  
    Mechanisms such as services and ingress resources control how traffic is routed internally and exposed externally.

- **Abstraction over infrastructure networking**  
    Kubernetes provides a consistent networking model independent of the underlying infrastructure or cloud provider.

- **Distributed system connectivity**  
    Networking forms a foundational layer for service discovery, load balancing, scalability, and reliable communication across distributed workloads.
