
A Forward Proxy is a proxy that **acts on behalf of clients when communicating with external servers or services**. It sits between clients and the internet, receiving requests from clients and forwarding them to their intended destinations.

---
### `Key Ideas`

- **Client-side intermediary**  
    Forward proxies represent clients rather than servers. External services see requests as originating from the proxy instead of the original client.
- **Traffic control and filtering**  
    Organizations commonly use forward proxies to enforce security policies, restrict access to websites, and monitor outbound traffic.
- **Privacy and anonymity**  
    By hiding the client's identity and IP address, forward proxies can provide varying degrees of privacy and anonymity.
- **Centralized network management**  
    Forward proxies allow administrators to manage internet access, caching, and logging from a single point within a network.
