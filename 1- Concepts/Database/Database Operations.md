
**Database Operations** refers to the activities and capabilities involved in maintaining, securing, and ensuring the reliability of a database system throughout its lifecycle. It focuses on keeping a database available, consistent, performant, and recoverable in real-world environments.

---
### `Key Ideas`

- **Lifecycle Management**  
    Databases require ongoing maintenance beyond initial setup, including upgrades, backups, and configuration adjustments.
    
- **Reliability & Recovery**  
    Systems must be protected against data loss through backups, replication, and recovery strategies.
    
- **Availability & Fault Tolerance**  
    Operational strategies ensure databases remain accessible even during hardware or network failures.
    
- **Performance Monitoring**  
    Observability tools track resource usage, query performance, and system health.
    
- **Access & Security Management**  
    Control of user permissions, authentication, and secure communication.
    
- **Operational Separation from Engine**  
    Unlike internal engine mechanisms, operations concern how the database is maintained and managed in production environments.
