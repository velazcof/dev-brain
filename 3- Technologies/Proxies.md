
Proxies are software technologies that **act as intermediaries between communicating systems**, receiving requests from one party and forwarding them to another on its behalf.

They abstract direct communication between clients and servers, enabling additional capabilities such as security, routing, monitoring, and traffic management.

---
### `Key Ideas`

- **Intermediary communication layer**  
    Proxies sit between two communicating entities, forwarding requests and responses while potentially inspecting or modifying traffic.
- **Traffic management capabilities**  
    Proxies can provide features such as access control, caching, filtering, logging, and protocol translation.
- **Communication abstraction**  
    By decoupling clients from servers, proxies improve flexibility, security, and scalability within networked systems.
- **Multiple proxy models**  
    Proxy technologies can operate on behalf of clients or servers, leading to specialized forms such as forward proxies and reverse proxies.

---
### `Use Cases`

- Hiding or protecting network endpoints
- Controlling and filtering traffic
- Improving security and observability
- Caching responses to improve performance
- Routing requests across distributed systems
- Acting as an entry point for web applications and services
