
A **Relational DBMS** is a database management system that implements the relational model for structuring and managing data. It organizes data into tables composed of rows and columns and supports structured querying through relational operations.

---
### `Key Ideas`

- **Table-Based Structure**  
    Data is stored in relations (tables) defined by schemas.
    
- **Structured Querying**  
    Typically exposes SQL as the primary interface for querying and manipulation.
    
- **Schema Enforcement**  
    Enforces predefined schemas, data types, and constraints.
    
- **Relational Operations**  
    Supports operations such as selection, projection, and joins to combine and filter data.
    
- **Transactional Support**  
    Commonly designed to support strong transactional guarantees.
    
- **Set-Oriented Processing**  
    Queries operate over sets of rows rather than individual records.

---
### `Use Cases`

- Transactional business systems
    
- Structured enterprise applications
    
- Financial systems requiring strong consistency
    
- Systems requiring complex relational queries
