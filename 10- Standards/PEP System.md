
The PEP System (Python Enhancement Proposal system) defines the **formal process for proposing, documenting, and standardizing changes to the Python language and ecosystem**. It establishes how new features, design decisions, and conventions are introduced, evaluated, and adopted within Python.

---
### `Purpose`

- Provide a **structured and transparent process** for evolving Python
- Ensure changes are **well-documented and justified**
- Enable **community discussion and review** of proposals
- Maintain consistency across **language design, standard library, and ecosystem practices**
- Serve as a **historical record of design decisions**

---
### `Structure`

- Each proposal is documented as a **PEP (Python Enhancement Proposal)**
    
- PEPs include sections such as:
    
    - abstract
    - motivation
    - specification
    - rationale
    
- PEPs go through defined **statuses**:
    
    - Draft
    - Accepted
    - Rejected
    - Final
    
- Reviewed by **Python core developers and the Steering Council**
    
- Categorized into types such as:
    
    - **Standards Track** (language and library changes)
    - **Informational** (guidelines and documentation)
    - **Process** (governance and workflow changes)
